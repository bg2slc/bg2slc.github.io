About Me

I first started getting into programming at 14 years old, and if I remember correctly I probably started with a turtle drawing language based on Turing. Since then I've learned to work with a variety of language such as Java, C++, C, C#, Scheme, Javascript, Lua, and Bash.

Since then, I've attended Carleton University in Ottawa, and while I didn't finish my program there I learned a lot about Computer Science, programming, operating systems, abstract data structures and algorithms, and it's where I first got started in GNU/Linux.

My first distro I ever touched was Fedora Core 4, after that Ubuntu 8.04, and then I challenged myself to use Slackware without dual-booting for a full year, and after that I was hooked. These days, I use Arch Linux on all of my personal systems, and only use Windows as required for work.

In the years after university, I've worked a very wide variety of non-coding jobs, everything from custodial work, to solar panel installation at solar farms, to an inbound customer and retail service rep for a couple of US telecoms. During most of this time, I continued to learn about Linux and programming.

In 2021 I got into the St Lawrence Computer Programming and Analysis advanced diploma program, to get a foothold in the industry. Later in the program, I applied for and was accepted for a co-op position at Novari Health, where I would later gain full-time employment after graduating.

I'm currently employed as an Implementations Developer at Novari Health, a lot of the projects listed here were written before I began work there.
